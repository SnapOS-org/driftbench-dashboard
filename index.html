How decision audits work

Every automated decision has an anatomy.

A decision is not just an output. It is a chain: input, policy, evidence, action, trace. DriftBench checks every link. Most systems break at the evidence and trace stage — exactly where regulators look first.

1 · INPUT
Transaction & Context
Customer, amount, geography, risk signals
2 · POLICY
Governing Rule
The rule that applies — with version and review date
3 · EVIDENCE
Witness Reference
Auditable link to the data or rule that triggered the decision
4 · DECISION
Action + Rationale
Block / Approve / Escalate — with stated reason
5 · AUDIT TRACE
Reproducible Record
Can a regulator reconstruct this decision in 6 months?
Most AI fraud systems today can demonstrate step 1 and 4.
Steps 2, 3, and 5 are where audit failures happen.
Why this matters

Black-box decisions have systemic consequences.

This is not a new problem. It is a recurring one — now accelerated by AI systems making thousands of decisions per minute.

2008 · Financial Crisis
Credit models assigned ratings without auditable rationale. When challenged, institutions could not explain individual decisions. Systemic risk was invisible until it was not.
2012 · Knight Capital
An automated trading system operated outside its authorized mandate for 45 minutes. Every dashboard showed green. $440M lost before a human could intervene. The system had no fail-closed mechanism.
2024 · EU AI Act
Art. 13 requires traceability. Art. 14 requires human oversight. Art. 9 requires risk management. Automated fraud decisions are high-risk by default. Non-compliance carries fines up to 3% of global turnover.
